What an Observation Day Looks Like

TDI coaches spend a full day in your buildings. We observe classrooms, watch how strategies are being used, and write personalized feedback to every educator we visit. This is not an evaluation. It is support.

Example Love Note

Love Note from TDI

To: Ms. Rivera, 3rd Grade

At 9:14 this morning, I watched you notice that Marcus had completely disengaged from the math warm-up. Instead of calling him out, you walked over, knelt next to his desk, and quietly asked "What part feels hard right now?" He pointed to the second problem. You worked it with him for 30 seconds and then stepped away. By 9:16 he was working independently.

That is proximity, redirection, and relationship-building in one seamless move. It took you 90 seconds. It changed his entire morning. You should know that what you did there is the kind of teaching that textbooks describe but rarely capture in action.

One thing to try: the next time you circulate during independent work, pause at two more desks and ask the same question, even if those students seem fine. You might be surprised what surfaces.

With genuine respect, TDI Coaching Team

What Happens When Someone Is Struggling

If an educator logs multiple tough days in a row, TDI CEO Rae Hughart personally sends them a check-in email. Not a form. Not a survey. Just a "hey, I see you, this job is hard, I am in your corner" message.

You will see "TDI reached out to X team members this month" on your dashboard. You will never see names. You just know someone has your teachers' backs.

Industry context: The national average teacher stress level is 8.9 out of 10. TDI partner schools average 5.2 out of 10 after one year. That is not a marginal improvement. That is a different experience of the job.
