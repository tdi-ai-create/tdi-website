Example Love Note

Love Note from TDI

To: Ms. Antonelli, 2nd Grade

At 10:22 this morning, I watched you handle something most teachers rush through. A student returned from the bathroom visibly upset. Without missing a beat, you continued the read-aloud with the class while walking toward her. You placed your hand gently on her shoulder, leaned down, and whispered something. She nodded. You slid a feelings check-in card onto her desk. By the time the chapter ended, she had written something on it and was ready to rejoin the conversation.

You kept 23 students engaged while simultaneously providing a private, dignified emotional check-in for one child. That is the kind of multi-layered teaching that looks effortless but requires deep skill. You should know that the seamlessness of that moment is something we rarely see, even in veteran classrooms.

One thing to try: consider keeping a small basket of those feelings check-in cards on each table group so students can self-select them without needing you to initiate. You might be surprised how many students start using them on their own.

With genuine respect, TDI Coaching Team

What Happens When Someone Is Struggling

If an educator logs multiple tough days in a row, TDI CEO Rae Hughart personally sends them a check-in email. Not a form. Not a survey. Just a "hey, I see you, this job is hard, I am in your corner" message.

You will see "TDI reached out to X team members this month" on your dashboard. You will never see names. You just know someone has your teachers' backs.
