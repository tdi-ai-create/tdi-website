Example Love Note

Love Note from TDI

To: Mrs. Thompson, 5th Grade

At 10:22 this morning, I watched you transition your class from reading groups back to whole-group instruction. You gave a quiet countdown, made eye contact with two students who were still settling, and had the entire room ready in under 45 seconds. No raised voice. No repeated directions. Just calm, practiced routine.

What stood out is that you did not rush the two students who needed an extra moment. You waited, gave a small nod, and they came along on their own. That is trust. Your students trust that you will give them the space to get there, and because of that trust, they get there faster.

One thing to try: during your next read-aloud, pause after a key moment and ask "What do you think happens next, and why?" Give 10 seconds of silent think time before taking responses. You might find that your quieter students start raising their hands.

With genuine respect, TDI Coaching Team
