The partnership lifecycle (your 5-step flow)

1
Close the deal in Sales

Track leads, schedule demos, send proposals. When the contract is signed, move the deal to "Signed" in the pipeline.

Your action

Click "Create Partnership + Dashboard" on the deal. Fill in staff count, observation days, sessions, contract dates. Hit Create.

System auto-does

Creates the Leadership Dashboard, provisions Hub access for the contact, sends a welcome email from you, creates 9 onboarding action items, logs everything. If grant-funded, also creates a funding pursuit with Plan A/B/C/D paths.

2
Onboard the school (Phase 0)

The principal sees action items on their dashboard. They upload their roster, identify a champion, and schedule a kickoff.

What you watch for

You get an email notification when the principal uploads their roster. Go to the Internal tab and click "Provision Hub accounts for all staff" to create their Hub access.

System auto-does

Sends the principal reminders. Tracks which items are complete. Fires attention flags if the principal hasn't logged in by Day 7 or staff are below 50% by Day 14. You get emailed when flags fire.

3
Set KPIs together

During your kickoff walkthrough, set 3-5 KPIs with the principal.

Your action

Go to Internal tab > Partnership KPIs. Click "Select KPIs." Pick from the curated menu (implementation-focused, with benchmarks). Set targets. Save.

System auto-does

The principal's dashboard gauge rings now show their KPIs instead of generic metrics. KPI values auto-update daily from Hub data (engagement, completions, field notes, PD hours). Survey-based KPIs (stress, retention) need manual update after surveys.

4
Manage the partnership year-round

The Internal tab is your command center. Notes, meetings, KPI tracking, grant status, exports, and call prep all live here.

Before every check-in

Click "Generate briefing" on the Internal tab. Opens a printable 1-page summary with engagement data, KPI progress, recent notes, and AI-suggested talking points.

After every check-in

Log the meeting on the Internal tab (date, type, attendees, summary, action items). These get referenced in your next briefing.

System auto-does

Every Monday, you get a weekly digest email with all partnerships: health status, Hub %, flags, pending items. The principal gets a monthly engagement email (AI-written in your voice) with their team's highlights and a link to their dashboard.

5
Renew

By Day 45, plant the Year 2 vision. By Day 80, check in on budget. By Day 90, deliver the impact report.

Your action

Use Export Reports on the Internal tab to generate a board summary, KPI standings, or full export. The AI Summary on the principal's dashboard is screenshot-ready for board presentations.

Things to be careful about

Creating a partnership is permanent. The "Create Partnership" button generates a dashboard, provisions Hub access, sends a welcome email, and creates action items. Make sure the deal is actually signed before clicking.
Batch provisioning creates real accounts. "Provision Hub accounts" creates All-Access Hub accounts for every person in the roster. Only run this after the roster is verified.
KPIs show on the principal's dashboard. Once you set KPIs, the principal sees gauge rings tracking progress. Pick KPIs that TDI can actually move (use the implementation category, not just login metrics).
Notes default to internal. Anything on the Internal tab is invisible to the principal. But if you mark a note as "shared with partner," it will be visible. Double-check before sharing.
