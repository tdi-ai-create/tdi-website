What This System Does

This is where we track every grant and funding path for every school we work with. TDI's promise is that we do 90% of the work -- we research funders, write narratives, draft emails, build submission packets. The school contact just needs to follow our instructions and hit send.

Your job: Make sure nothing falls through the cracks. Every school contact has clear next steps, every deadline is tracked, and every stalled item gets a nudge.

How to use it:

  • Filter tabs let you focus: All, Overdue, Client, or TDI
  • Checkbox on the left -- click it when a task is done
  • "Nudge" button (client tasks only) -- sends a pre-written email to the school contact automatically. One click. You don't write anything.
  • Orange "2x" badge -- shows how many times they've been nudged. If 3x+ and nothing happened, flag it for Rae.
  • Red highlighting -- task is overdue. Deal with these first.
Start each day by filtering to "Overdue" and working through that list.

Actions Tab

Same as the main Action Items view, but filtered to just this school. Split into Client Tasks (shown first) and TDI Tasks. You can add new tasks here too.

Timeline Tab

Chronological history of everything that's happened. Events are auto-created when emails are sent, actions are completed, or statuses change.

Emails Tab

Compose emails using pre-written templates, or view the full email log for this school.

  • Pick a template type (nudge, submission instructions, deadline reminder, status update, follow-up)
  • The system auto-fills school name, contact name, funding source, deadline, etc.
  • Save Draft to review later, or Send Now to send immediately

Adding a New School

When a new school signs on and needs funding support:

1
Click "+ Add Pursuit" at the top right
2
Fill in the form
School name, contract amount, implementation date (usually August), and client contact info (name, email, phone, role)
3
Click "Create pursuit"
The system does the rest.
What happens automatically:
  • 6 standard funding opportunities are created (Title II-A, IDEA/CEIS, Title I, NEA, Community Schools, Walmart)
  • 9 action items are created with due dates calculated from the implementation date
  • All TDI tasks are assigned to you
  • A timeline event is logged

After creation, customize: remove opportunities that don't apply to this school, add any school-specific ones, and start working through the action items.

5 Rules to Live By

1
Never let a client task sit more than 7 days without a nudge.
If it's overdue and you've nudged 2+ times, tell Rae.
2
Update statuses as things change.
When a client confirms submission, change the opportunity to "applied" or "waiting" and check off the action item.
3
Log everything in the timeline.
Phone calls, status changes, anything notable. If it's not in the system, it didn't happen.
4
Check the Overdue filter first thing every morning.
That's where money gets left on the table.
5
When in doubt, nudge.
The emails are pre-written and friendly. One too many is better than letting a $33,000 grant expire.
