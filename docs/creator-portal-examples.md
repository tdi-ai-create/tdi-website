## 2. Learning Objectives Examples

### What Makes a Strong Learning Objective?

Learning objectives should be:
- **Specific** - Clear about what learners will do
- **Measurable** - You can assess if they achieved it
- **Action-oriented** - Uses active verbs
- **Realistic** - Achievable within the lesson/module

### Strong vs. Weak Learning Objectives

| Weak Objective | Why It's Weak | Strong Objective |
|----------------|---------------|------------------|
| "Understand classroom management" | Too vague, can't measure "understand" | "Identify 3 proactive strategies to prevent disruptions before they start" |
| "Learn about parent communication" | "Learn about" is passive | "Write a parent email using the positive-first framework" |
| "Know different behavior strategies" | "Know" can't be observed | "Select appropriate de-escalation techniques for 3 common scenarios" |
| "Be familiar with routines" | "Be familiar" is unmeasurable | "Design a 10-minute morning routine for your classroom" |

### Action Verbs for Learning Objectives

**Knowledge/Remember:** identify, list, name, recognize, recall, define

**Comprehension/Understand:** describe, explain, summarize, interpret, compare

**Application/Apply:** use, demonstrate, implement, create, design, develop

**Analysis/Analyze:** differentiate, distinguish, examine, compare, contrast

**Evaluation/Evaluate:** assess, critique, judge, justify, recommend, select

**Creation/Create:** design, construct, develop, formulate, produce, plan

### Example Objectives by Module Type

**Introductory Module:**
- Identify the 3 key principles of [topic]
- Explain why [concept] matters for [audience]
- Recognize common misconceptions about [topic]

**Strategy/Technique Module:**
- Demonstrate the [technique name] in a classroom scenario
- Apply the [framework] to plan a lesson activity
- Use the [strategy] to respond to [situation]

**Application Module:**
- Create a personalized [plan/template] for your classroom
- Design a [routine/system] based on the principles covered
- Develop an action plan for implementing [strategy] this week

**Wrap-up Module:**
- Evaluate your current practices against the [framework]
- Select 2-3 strategies to implement immediately
- Create a 30-day plan for sustained implementation

## 3. Download Definition Examples

When defining your download, you'll answer these questions:
1. What is the title of your resource?
2. What format will it take?
3. Who is it for?
4. What will educators walk away with?

### Example 1: Checklist

**Title:** First Week Survival Checklist

**Format:** One-page printable checklist (PDF)

**Target Audience:** First-year teachers or teachers new to a grade level

**What educators will walk away with:** A day-by-day checklist covering everything from setting up the physical classroom to establishing routines, building relationships, and surviving the emotional rollercoaster of week one. Teachers will feel organized, prepared, and less anxious knowing they haven't forgotten anything important.

---

### Example 2: Template Collection

**Title:** Parent Communication Templates

**Format:** Multi-page Google Doc/PDF with copy-paste templates

**Target Audience:** K-8 classroom teachers who want to save time on parent communication

**What educators will walk away with:** Ready-to-use email and note templates for 10 common scenarios: welcome letter, weekly updates, positive behavior notes, behavior concerns, missing homework, conference requests, absence follow-up, event reminders, end-of-year message, and difficult conversation openers. Each template includes placeholders for personalization and can be adapted in under 2 minutes.

---

### Example 3: Planning Guide

**Title:** The Ultimate Classroom Reset Guide

**Format:** 8-10 page guide with reflection questions and planning pages

**Target Audience:** Teachers returning from winter or spring break feeling like they need to "start over"

**What educators will walk away with:** A step-by-step process for resetting classroom expectations, routines, and culture mid-year. Includes reflection questions to identify what's not working, a priority matrix for choosing what to tackle first, scripts for re-teaching expectations, and a 2-week implementation calendar. Teachers will feel empowered to make a fresh start without feeling like they've failed.

---

### Example 4: Toolkit/Resource Bundle

**Title:** SEL Morning Meeting Toolkit

**Format:** Multi-page resource bundle with activities, printables, and planning guides

**Target Audience:** Elementary teachers who want to incorporate social-emotional learning into their daily routine

**What educators will walk away with:** Everything needed to run engaging 10-15 minute morning meetings for an entire semester. Includes 50 greeting ideas, 30 sharing prompts organized by SEL competency, 25 quick activities, a planning template, and a tracking sheet for ensuring variety. Teachers can open the toolkit any morning and have a meaningful meeting ready in seconds.

---

### Example 5: Quick Reference Guide

**Title:** Behavior Response Quick Reference Card

**Format:** One-page (front and back) desk reference card

**Target Audience:** K-5 teachers who want in-the-moment strategies at their fingertips

**What educators will walk away with:** A laminate-ready reference card with color-coded behavior response strategies organized by behavior level (green = minor, yellow = moderate, red = major). Each level includes 3-4 specific strategies with quick implementation tips. Teachers can glance at the card during instruction and immediately select an appropriate response.

---

### Example 6: Self-Assessment Tool

**Title:** Classroom Management Self-Assessment

**Format:** Interactive self-assessment with reflection guide (4-6 pages)

**Target Audience:** Teachers in years 2-10 who want to identify growth areas

**What educators will walk away with:** A comprehensive self-assessment covering 5 domains of classroom management (relationships, routines, environment, instruction, and behavior response) with a scoring rubric and personalized next steps based on results. Teachers will gain clarity on their strengths and a prioritized action plan for improvement.

5 RULES FOR PARENT EMAILS:

### What We're Looking For

Your test video helps us ensure your recording setup produces quality that meets TDI standards. Here's what we check:

### Video Quality Checklist

#### Lighting
| Requirement | What to Check |
|-------------|---------------|
| Face clearly visible | No shadows across your face |
| Even lighting | Not too bright on one side, dark on the other |
| No backlight glare | Window or bright light shouldn't be behind you |
| Natural appearance | Not washed out or overly dark |

**Quick Fix:** Position yourself facing a window with natural light, or use a ring light in front of you.

#### Audio
| Requirement | What to Check |
|-------------|---------------|
| Clear voice | Words are easy to understand |
| Consistent volume | Not too quiet or distorted |
| No echo | Room doesn't sound hollow |
| Minimal background noise | No fans, traffic, pets, etc. |

**Quick Fix:** Use an external microphone if possible. Record in a room with soft furniture (absorbs sound). Close windows and turn off fans.

#### Framing
| Requirement | What to Check |
|-------------|---------------|
| Head and shoulders visible | Not cut off at forehead or chin |
| Eye level camera | Camera at or slightly above eye level |
| Appropriate headroom | Small space above head, not too much |
| Clean background | No clutter or distracting items |

**Quick Fix:** Stack books under your laptop to raise the camera. Sit an arm's length from the camera. Check what's visible behind you.

```

[Pause for 2-3 seconds - this helps us check for audio delay]

[describe your equipment - laptop camera, external webcam, etc.]
and [microphone type - built-in, USB mic, lapel mic, etc.].

Let me give you a quick preview of my course content:

[Share 30-60 seconds of actual course content - teach something

For example, one strategy I'll share in my course is...
[brief teaching moment]

[Return to normal talking]

That's a quick preview! I'm excited to create this course and

```

1. Submit your test video link
2. TDI team reviews within 2-3 business days

   - **Approved!** You're ready to start recording

### Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Likely Cause | Solution |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| Dark or shadowy face | Light source behind you | Move light in front of you |
| Grainy/pixelated video | Low light or camera quality | Add more lighting; check camera settings |
| Echo or hollow sound | Hard surfaces in room | Add soft items (blankets, pillows); record in closet |
| Inconsistent audio levels | Moving away from mic | Stay consistent distance from microphone |
| Looking down at camera | Laptop on desk | Raise laptop to eye level |
| Cluttered background | Visible mess | Simplify background or use virtual background |

## 6. Recording Tips Guide

### Before You Record

**Environment Setup**
- [ ] Choose a quiet space where you won't be interrupted
- [ ] Turn off phone notifications
- [ ] Put a "Recording - Do Not Disturb" sign on your door
- [ ] Close windows to reduce outside noise
- [ ] Turn off fans, AC units, or other noise-makers if possible
- [ ] Have water nearby (but off-camera!)

**Technical Setup**
- [ ] Camera at eye level (stack books if needed)
- [ ] Good lighting on your face (natural light or ring light)
- [ ] Microphone tested and positioned correctly
- [ ] Background clean and not distracting
- [ ] Computer plugged in (don't rely on battery)
- [ ] Close unnecessary programs to prevent slowdown

**Content Prep**
- [ ] Review your outline for this lesson
- [ ] Have bullet points or script visible (but don't read word-for-word)
- [ ] Prepare any materials you'll reference or show
- [ ] Know your first sentence (hardest part is starting!)

### During Recording

**Delivery Tips**
- Speak slightly slower than feels natural
- Pause between main points (easy edit points)
- Look at the camera lens, not the screen
- It's okay to gesture naturally
- Smile when appropriate - energy translates through video
- If you mess up, pause, take a breath, and restart that sentence

**Ideal Video Length**
| Content Type | Recommended Length |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Welcome/Intro | 2-4 minutes |
| Core lesson | 5-10 minutes |
| Strategy deep-dive | 7-12 minutes |
| Wrap-up/Summary | 2-4 minutes |

*Shorter is almost always better. If a lesson runs 15+ minutes, consider splitting it.*

**If You Make a Mistake**
1. Don't panic or apologize to the camera
2. Pause for 2-3 seconds (creates clear edit point)
3. Start the sentence again from the beginning
4. Continue as normal
5. Our editors will cut the mistake (or you can if self-editing)

### After Recording

**File Naming Convention**
```
[Module#]-[Lesson#]-[ShortTitle].mp4

Examples:
1-1-Welcome.mp4
1-2-ThreePillars.mp4
2-1-MorningRoutines.mp4
2-2-Transitions.mp4
```

**Quality Check Before Submitting**
- [ ] Watch at least the first minute - is audio clear?
- [ ] Spot-check the middle - is lighting consistent?
- [ ] Watch the last 30 seconds - did you end cleanly?
- [ ] Is the file readable/playable?

### Recording Multiple Takes

It's normal to need multiple takes! Here's how to stay organized:

**Option 1: One file, multiple attempts**
Record straight through. Pause between attempts. Let editor cut.

**Option 2: Separate files per attempt**
Name files: `1-2-ThreePillars-Take1.mp4`, `1-2-ThreePillars-Take2.mp4`
Note which take is best when submitting.

### Complete Example Blog Draft

```
BLOG DRAFT
Author: [Your Name]
Proposed Title: 5 Morning Meeting Games That Actually Work
                (And Take Under 5 Minutes)
Word Count: ~950 words

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[TITLE: H1]
5 Morning Meeting Games That Actually Work (And Take Under 5 Minutes)

[Intro paragraph - hook the reader]
I'll be honest: I used to skip morning meeting more often than
I'd like to admit. Between taking attendance, collecting homework,
and putting out the first fires of the day, spending 20 minutes
on a greeting and share felt like a luxury I couldn't afford.

Then I learned something that changed everything: effective
morning meetings don't need to take 20 minutes. Five minutes
is enough to build community, set a positive tone, and connect
with your students.

Here are five of my favorite quick morning meeting games that
my students BEG to play.

[H2]
1. The Silent Greeting

**Time: 2 minutes**

Have students greet each other without speaking. They might
wave, give a thumbs up, do a silent high-five, or create their
own signature silent greeting with a partner.

**Why it works:** It's calm, it's controlled, and it forces
students to make eye contact and actually SEE each other.
Perfect for those mornings when energy is already too high.

**Try this:** Challenge students to greet at least 5 classmates
in 60 seconds using only silent greetings.

[H2]
2. Would You Rather (Lightning Round)

**Time: 3 minutes**

Pose a "would you rather" question and have students move to
one side of the room or the other based on their answer.
Then ask 1-2 students from each side to share their reasoning.

**Why it works:** Movement + opinion sharing + low-stakes
participation = engagement for even your most reluctant students.

**Sample questions:**
- Would you rather have recess all morning or all afternoon?
- Would you rather be able to fly or be invisible?
- Would you rather have a pet dinosaur or a pet dragon?

[NOTE: Should we create a small callout box for the sample
questions? Might look nice visually.]

[H2]
3. Compliment Circle Speed Round

**Time: 4 minutes**

Students stand in a circle. Going around quickly, each student
gives a one-word compliment to the person on their right.
Encourage words like: kind, helpful, funny, creative, brave,
smart, caring.

**Why it works:** Receiving a compliment releases dopamine.
Starting the day by hearing something positive about yourself?
That's powerful.

**Variation:** Theme the compliments - "Give a compliment about
something you noticed this week" or "Compliment something
about their work."

[H2]
4. Rose & Thorn Express

**Time: 3-4 minutes**

A quick version of the classic Rose & Thorn sharing. Students
share in one sentence: "My rose is ___ and my thorn is ___."

Call on 4-5 students randomly (popsicle sticks work great) or
do a quick whip-around where every student shares.

**Why it works:** You get a pulse check on your class in under
4 minutes. You'll know who's having a rough day before problems
start.

**Pro tip:** I keep a small notebook where I jot down students
who mention significant thorns. It helps me remember to check
in with them later.

[H2]
5. Freeze Dance: Scholar Edition

**Time: 3-4 minutes**

Play music and let students dance. When the music stops, call
out an academic prompt and students must freeze in a pose that
represents the answer.

Examples:
- "Show me an acute angle with your arms!"
- "Freeze in a pose that shows an emotion from our read-aloud!"
- "Make the shape of the first letter of a vocabulary word!"

**Why it works:** Movement, music, and academics combined.
Plus it's hilarious to watch, and laughter is never a waste
of time.

[H2]
Ready for More?

These five games are just the beginning. If you're looking for
a full semester of morning meeting activities that take 10
minutes or less, check out my SEL Morning Meeting Toolkit.
It includes 50 greeting ideas, 30 sharing prompts, and 25
quick activities - all organized by SEL competency.

[LINK to download: SEL Morning Meeting Toolkit]

[Closing paragraph]
Morning meeting doesn't have to be a time-consuming production.
Start with just one of these games tomorrow. Your students will
love it, you'll build community, and you'll still have time for
everything else on your plate.

Five minutes. Every day. That's all it takes.

---

[NOTE: I'll provide a headshot. Here's my bio:]

[Your Name] is a [grade level] teacher in [City, State] with
[X years] of experience. She's passionate about building
classroom community through social-emotional learning. When
she's not teaching, you can find her [personal detail].

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```
