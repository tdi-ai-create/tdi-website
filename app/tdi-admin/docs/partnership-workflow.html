1. System overview

The lifecycle of a TDI partnership

Every school moves through one pipeline: Sales (close the deal) to Leadership (manage the partnership) to Hub (educator engagement). Operations sits behind Leadership as the internal documentation layer. Funding manages grant work for grant-supported schools. The principal sees their Leadership Dashboard. Your team sees everything.

1
Sales -- Lead, demos, proposal, contract signed
↓
2
Deal signed (automation) -- One click creates partnership, dashboard, Hub access, onboarding items, welcome email. Grant-funded deals also create a funding pursuit.
↓
3
Phase 0: Pre-launch -- Roster, photos, champion, kickoff, Hub provisioning, KPI setting
↓
4
Phase 1-4: Active partnership -- Days 1-90 framework, Hub engagement, observations, virtual sessions
↓
5
Renewal -- AI summary, KPI progress, engagement data, Year 2 vision

2. Deal signed: the automation

When you mark a deal as Signed (w/o Grant) or Signed (w/ Grant) in Sales, a "Create Partnership + Dashboard" button appears.

Modal fields

FieldPre-filled?Default
School/district name, contactYes (from deal)--
Partnership typeNoSchool
Staff count (Hub memberships)No0
Observation days / Virtual sessions / Executive sessionsNo2 / 4 / 2
Contract start/end datesNoToday / empty
Building count (districts)No1

What happens on click

  1. Partnership record created in Leadership
  2. Organization record created (name, city, state, website)
  3. Dashboard generated at /partners/[slug]
  4. 9 onboarding action items created (5 partner-visible, 4 admin-only)
  5. Hub All-Access provisioned for contact
  6. Welcome email sent from Rae
  7. Sales deal linked to partnership
  8. Activity logged
  9. If grant-funded: Funding pursuit auto-created with Plan A/B/C/D paths + 4 grant action items

API: POST /api/admin/deal-to-partnership

4. What the principal sees

Client-facing dashboard at /partners/[slug]

  • AI Summary -- one paragraph, real-time
  • Gauge rings -- KPI-driven when set (shows their actual targets + progress), generic fallback (Hub %, Deliverables, Wellness, Phase)
  • Partnership Health bar -- momentum calculated from deliverables + Hub + pending items
  • Quick Actions -- Open Hub, Schedule, Quiz, Tools
  • Popular Tools + Educator Types
  • Teacher quote
  • Collapsible sections -- Hub Intelligence, Timeline, Data & Impact, Actions, Leadership Tools, Community & FAQ
  • Interactive action items -- upload roster, schedule walkthrough, identify champion

Internal tab sections

  1. Partnership KPIs -- pick 3-5 from curated menu, set targets, see benchmarks
  2. Grant tracking -- shows linked funding pursuits with Plan A/B/C/D status (only for grant-funded partnerships)
  3. Hub provisioning -- one-click batch create Hub accounts for all roster staff
  4. Reports and exports -- 7 report types: roster, board summary, KPI standings, engagement, courses, wellness (anonymized), full export. Smart empty states with email CTAs.

  6. Meetings -- date, type, attendees, summary, action items. Types: check-in, onboarding, observation debrief, renewal, strategy, escalation.

7. Hub educator goals

Teachers set 3-5 personal goals in their Hub profile (Growth tab > "Get specific" section). 1-2 can be mandated by the district.

Guided prompts (5 categories)

  1. Classroom: "What is one strategy you want to consistently use?"
  2. Growth: "What skill do you want to develop?"
  3. Wellness: "What will you do to protect your well-being?"
  4. Connection: "How will you connect with colleagues?"
  5. Impact: "What student outcome do you want to improve?"

Quarterly check-ins: August, October, January, March, May. Teachers review and adjust goals.

Aggregated goal data available in Leadership Dashboard exports.

API: GET/POST /api/hub/goals

8. Grant-funded partnerships

When a deal moves to "Signed (w/ Grant)", the automation additionally creates a funding pursuit in the Funding tab.

Funding Playbook integration

Based on the TDI Funding Playbook v2. Every pursuit starts with default Plan A/B/C/D paths:

PlanTypeDefault paths
AFederal formulaTitle II-A, IDEA/CEIS, Title I Part A
BState formulaState discretionary dollars
CFoundation/corporateCompetitive applications
DCrowdfunding/directTeacher-led, local sponsors

What's tracked per path

Label, amount, status, deadline, contact, notes. Editable inline on the pursuit detail panel.

Status options: not started, researching, pursuing, submitted, awarded, denied, on hold.

Where grant data appears

  • Funding tab -- full pursuit management with phase tracking
  • Leadership Internal tab -- grant tracking section shows linked pursuit with Plan A/B/C/D status
  • Admin action items -- 4 grant-specific items auto-created

API: POST/PATCH /api/funding/pursuits

12. Partner attention flags

Daily cron (8 AM CT) checks all active partnerships within their first 90 days:

SignalThresholdWhen
Principal not logged in0 loginsDay 7
Staff below target<50% logged inDay 14
Principal still not logged in0 logins in 14 daysDay 21 (escalation)
Active usage below floor<40% staff activeDay 45+

Flags create internal "concern" notes on the partnership's Internal tab. Seasonal threshold extensions apply during testing windows and breaks.

13. Renewal flow

  • Day 45: Plant Year 2 vision (not a renewal ask)

Attention flagsDailyChecks Day 7/14/21/45 thresholds for all partnerships in first 90 days. Creates internal concern notes + sends admin email.
