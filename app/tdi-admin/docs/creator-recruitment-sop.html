Creator Recruitment Workflow

How we identify content gaps, find creators, and move them from candidate to onboarded:driven by what schools are buying and what the Hub needs.

Last updated: July 19, 2026

Overview

Creator recruitment is not a separate activity:it's driven by content gaps in the Hub, informed by what schools are actually buying, and executed through the same approve pattern Bella uses for feedback.

Anne Marie Identifies Gaps → Anne Marie Researches Candidates → Anne Marie Drafts Outreach → Bella Approves & Sends → Bella Nurtures → Convert to Creator
Golden Rule: We recruit for gaps that matter:not to hit a number. Every candidate in the pipeline should be tied to a content gap that schools are asking for or the Hub needs.

Phase 1: Identify Gaps (Anne Marie, weekly)

Every Monday, Anne Marie generates a gap report by cross-referencing three data sources:

Source 1: Hub Content Inventory

What courses and quick wins exist in each category, and where are the holes?

Source 2: Sales Pipeline Pain Points

What are the 99+ leads in the CRM actually asking for? If 15 schools mention paraprofessional PD and we have 2 courses, that's a higher priority gap than a category nobody asked about.

Source 3: Hub Engagement Data

Which categories have high completion rates (users want more)? Which have users searching but finding nothing?

The gap report prioritizes based on demand, not just supply. "15 active leads mention para training + 0 courses" is CRITICAL. "We have no Technology Integration course + 0 leads asked for it" is LOW.

Gap Priority Levels

CRITICAL:Active sales leads asking for this content AND no courses/creators covering it. Revenue impact.
HIGH:Hub users engage heavily in this category but content is thin. OR existing creator covering this gap is stalled 30+ days.
MEDIUM:Category exists but is underrepresented. No immediate sales pressure but would strengthen the Hub.

Content Path Recommendation

The gap report should recommend the fastest way to fill each gap:

  • Quick Win / Download (2-3 weeks): Best for fast fills while searching for a course creator
  • Blog (1-2 weeks): Thought leadership, drives SEO, low commitment for the creator
  • Course (3-6 months): The real investment. Only recruit for courses when you have a strong candidate willing to commit
Strategy: For every CRITICAL gap, recommend both a fast fill (download/quick win creator) AND a long-term fill (course creator). Don't wait 6 months for a course when a download can start helping schools next month.

Phase 2: Research Candidates (Anne Marie, automated)

For each priority gap, Anne Marie researches candidates from multiple sources:

SourceHow Anne Marie Finds ThemQuality Signal
Hub Power Users High lesson completion, streaks, field notes earned, active in gap category STRONG:they already know and use TDI content. Warm lead.
Substack Readers Engaged subscribers who reply, share, or comment on gap-related topics GOOD:they're in the TDI ecosystem. Warm-ish.
Social Media Teachers with following who create content in gap areas (TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn) VARIABLE:follower count doesn't equal teaching quality. Need evaluation step.
Sales Nominations Rae or Bella captures "this teacher is amazing" during a sales call or event STRONG:personal recommendation from someone who's seen their work.
Referrals Existing creators recommend someone they know STRONG:peer validation.
Inbound Interest Teachers who email asking "can I create content for TDI?" GOOD:self-selected motivation. Still need to evaluate fit.

Candidate Profile (drafted by Anne Marie)

For each candidate, Anne Marie produces:

  • Name, email, school/org, role
  • Source:where we found them and why
  • Expertise area:what they'd create content about
  • Gap filled:which content gap this addresses + priority level
  • Content path recommendation:course, download, or blog
  • Why they're a fit:specific evidence (engagement stats, content samples, referral context)
  • Social/web presence:handles, follower counts, content samples
  • Suggested outreach approach:which template, what to personalize
  • Drafted outreach message:ready for Bella to approve/edit

Phase 3: Outreach (Bella approves, sends manually)

Anne Marie drafts the outreach. Bella reviews on the Action Center (or Recruitment tab) and clicks one of three buttons:

1 Approve Outreach

What happens when you click:

  • The outreach text copies to your clipboard automatically
  • If the candidate has an email: a Gmail compose window opens with the draft pre-filled as the body
  • If no email but a social profile exists: their social profile opens in a new tab so you can DM them
  • If neither: you'll see a toast saying the text was copied. Find their contact info and paste.

You review the pre-filled message, make any final tweaks, and hit send yourself. The system does not auto-send.

2 Edit & Approve

Opens an inline editor with Anne Marie's draft. Rewrite it in your voice, then click Save. Same clipboard + contact flow as above.

3 Dismiss

Not a good fit. Candidate is archived. They won't be re-suggested.

4 After sending, click "Mark as Sent"

On the Recruitment tab, the candidate now shows in "Outreach Approved" stage. Click Mark as Sent to move them to "Outreach Sent" and start the auto follow-up timer.

The system never sends outreach on your behalf. It copies the text and opens the right channel. You always click send yourself in Gmail or the social platform. This keeps the human voice authentic.

Outreach varies by content path

Content PathPitch FocusCommitment Ask
Download / Quick Win "Turn your best strategy into a downloadable resource" 2-3 weeks, low lift, 50% revenue share
Blog "Share your expertise with our educator community" One article, ~1 week, visibility + affiliate link

Tip: For social media finds or cold outreach, start by suggesting a download or blog first. It's a lower barrier. If they do well, invite them to create a course. Don't lead with a 6-month commitment to someone who's never heard of TDI.

Automated Follow-ups (non-responsive candidates)

If a candidate doesn't respond to initial outreach, the system auto-sends:

  • Day 5: Gentle follow-up ("Just bumping this in case it got buried")
  • Day 12: Final touch ("No worries if the timing isn't right:just wanted to make sure you saw this")
  • Day 19: Auto-archive as "No Response"

Bella does NOT need to manage these manually. The system handles it.

Interested Response Template

When a candidate responds positively, send this (copy, personalize the first line, paste):

Thanks so much for getting back to me! I'm really glad this resonated with you. Here's a quick overview of what creating with TDI looks like: https://www.teachersdeserveit.com/create-with-us The short version: you bring the expertise, we handle everything else (editing, design, production, distribution). You earn 50% of revenue, paid monthly, forever. No deadline pressure. Most creators start with a downloadable resource (takes about 2-3 weeks) and some go on to build full courses. If you want to get a feel for what's on the Hub: https://www.teachersdeserveit.com/hub Would you be up for a quick 15-minute call this week? I can answer any questions and we can talk about what you'd want to create. Looking forward to it! Bella

Personalize the first line. Reference what they said in their response. "I loved hearing about your formative assessment framework" is better than a generic opener.

The Evaluation Step

Important: Before committing to a course creator, ask for a lightweight sample. A teacher with 10K followers might produce terrible course materials. A quiet Hub user might be brilliant. The evaluation step filters before you invest 3-6 months.

Evaluation options (pick one based on content path):

  • Course: "Record a 5-minute video explaining your favorite classroom strategy"
  • Download: "Send us a rough draft of the resource you'd create"
  • Blog: "Write a 500-word draft on the topic you'd cover"

This is reviewed in the Recruitment tab, not the Creator Studio. They don't become a creator until they pass evaluation and Bella clicks Convert.

Phase 5: Convert & Handoff (one click)

When Bella clicks Convert to Creator:

  1. Creator account auto-created with name, email, topic, content path
  2. Welcome email fires
  3. Milestones generated based on content path
  4. Anne Marie begins health monitoring (feedback loop is ready)
  5. Candidate disappears from Recruitment tab, appears in Creator Studio
The handoff is seamless. Everything Bella learned during recruitment (expertise, topic, content path, evaluation sample) carries into the creator profile. No re-entering information.

Pipeline Health (not volume targets)

We track pipeline health, not outreach volume. "Every CRITICAL gap has at least one candidate in pipeline" is the goal:not "send 5 emails per week." Volume targets on recruitment hurt quality.

What to track

MetricWhat It Tells YouHealthy Range
CRITICAL gaps with 0 candidates Are we ignoring what schools are buying? 0:every critical gap should have at least one candidate
Candidates in "Interested" stage Is the pipeline moving? 3-5 at any time
Days in pipeline (avg) Are candidates stalling? <21 days from outreach to committed/declined
Conversion rate (interested → committed) Is our pitch working? >30%
Source quality (which sources convert best) Where should Anne Marie focus? Track over time:adjust research priorities quarterly

Feedback Loop: Published Content Back to Recruitment

When a creator's content goes live on the Hub, track its engagement. This feeds back into recruitment intelligence:

  • High engagement: "Creators recruited from Hub power users have 3x higher content engagement than social media finds." → Weight future research toward Hub users.
  • Low engagement: "This creator's course has low completion rates despite good sales." → Review evaluation criteria. What did we miss?
  • Gap filled: Remove the gap from the priority list. Move to monitoring.
