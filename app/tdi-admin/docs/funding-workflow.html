Funding System Workflow

How TDI manages grant pursuits from school enrollment through award allocation.

Last updated: July 14, 2026 | Created by Rae Hughart

The Big Picture

School Enrolled → Research Grants → Write Narratives → QA + Approve → Deliver to School → School Submits → Award Decision → Allocate Funds

The funding system coordinates three groups: Bella manages the pipeline and approves work, Agents (Vanessa, Amara) research and draft, and Schools provide information and submit applications.

Bella's Daily Workflow

Step 1: Open the Work Queue

Go to Funding > My Queue. This is your command center. It shows four buckets:

BucketWhat's in it
Needs BellaItems waiting for your action -- start here every day
Needs RaeItems only Rae can handle (escalations, approvals)
With AgentsWork the AI agents are doing (drafting, researching)
Waiting on SchoolItems where the school needs to respond

Items are sorted by urgency. A red dot means critical (overdue or deadline this week). Blue is normal. Gray is low priority.

Step 2: Work the Queue Top-to-Bottom

Each item has an action button. Here's what they do:

Send to QASends a completed draft to Julie for quality review

Tip: After every action, a green confirmation message appears in the bottom-right corner so you know it worked.

Step 3: Check the Dashboard Alerts

The four cards at the top of the Funding page show at-a-glance priorities:

  • Waiting on Client -- how many opportunities need the school to act
  • Deadlines This Week -- applications closing in the next 7 days
  • Overdue Actions -- action items past their due date
  • Awarded YTD -- total grant dollars awarded this year

Cards with a red or orange left border need attention. Green means everything's on track.

How Agent Drafting Works

When Bella clicks Request draft on an opportunity:

  1. The portal sets the opportunity status to "requested" and assigns it to Vanessa (or Amara)
  2. On her next check-in, Vanessa picks up the assignment automatically
  3. Vanessa drafts the narrative using TDI's grant knowledge base for that specific funder
  4. Vanessa creates a Google Doc in the "TDI Grant Narratives" Drive folder
  5. Vanessa pushes the Doc link + plain text back to the portal
  6. The draft appears in the opportunity's narrative panel for review
  7. Bella reviews, sends to QA (Julie), then approves
Safety rules: Agents never mark a narrative as "ready" -- that is always Bella's approval. Agents never send anything directly to a school. All client-facing sends are human-gated.

The Alignment Gate

Before any outreach can happen for a pursuit, four checks must pass:

CheckWhat It Means
Submitter namedA school contact is designated to submit applications
Backup namedA second contact in case the submitter is unavailable
Admin sponsor namedA school admin who approved participation
Employment verifiedConfirmed the submitter still works at the school

The gate shows OPEN (green) or BLOCKED (red) on the pursuit detail page. Agents cannot draft for a pursuit with a blocked gate.

The Follow-Up Engine

An automated system monitors all pending action items and sends reminders on a schedule:

  • 3-5 business days before due: Sends a reminder to the assigned person
  • After due date: Sends daily escalating nudges
  • Escalation ladder: Contact → Backup → Admin Sponsor → Rae

Three Safety Gates

Every email must pass all three gates before it sends:

GateWhat It ChecksCurrent Status
DRY_RUN gateMaster switch. When ON, emails are logged but not actually sent.ON (no sends)
Window gateOnly sends for opportunities where the grant funding window is open.Active
Allowlist gateOnly sends to approved email addresses.TDI staff only

Email Tones

  • Client emails (to schools): Warm, from Bella, encouraging, with clear next steps
  • Internal emails (to Rae/team): Crisp, data-focused, action-oriented

Two-Contract Model

Each pursuit uses two contracts:

Contract 1 -- Minimum (Starting Point Agreement)
The guaranteed scope, paid from the school's own budget. This is what the school gets regardless of grant outcomes.

Contract 2 -- Grant Funded (Master Service Agreement)
The expanded scope, contingent on grant awards. Each line item (PD sessions, memberships, books, campus visits) has a price that agents use when writing grant narratives.

The Remaining Gap metric on pursuit cards shows how much of Contract 2 still needs funding. The progress bar fills as grants are awarded.

Go-Live Checklist

Before taking the system live with real schools:
  • Flip DRY_RUN to false in the follow-up engine
  • Add client email addresses to the send allowlist
  • Verify the agent loop works end-to-end (find_work → draft → Google Doc → push back)
  • Bella reviews and approves the email templates
  • Test a nudge send to a TDI staff address
  • Confirm the Alignment Gate blocks correctly when incomplete
  • Review the escalation ladder contacts for each pursuit
